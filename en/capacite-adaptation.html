💪 Resilience

Haiti's Adaptation Capacity

A people who have survived slavery, earthquakes, and multiple crises through extraordinary resilience.

Home / Adaptation Capacity

A History of Survival

Historical Resilience

From the revolution against slavery to the natural disasters of the 21st century, the Haitian people have demonstrated a remarkable capacity for resilience. Every ordeal has been overcome through deep collective determination and an unshakeable faith in the future.

Mutual Aid

Social Solidarity

Haitian mechanisms of social solidarity, such as the "konbit" (collective labor) and the "sòl" (community tontine), illustrate the people's capacity to organize collectively in the face of hardship. This solidarity is a profound cultural strength.

Facing Crises

Adapting to Crises

After the 2010 earthquake, millions of Haitians rebuilt their lives with minimal resources. The Haitian diaspora, community organizations, and individual creativity played a crucial role in this reconstruction.

Beyond Resilience

The Limits of Resilience Without Institutional Support

While Haitian resilience is admirable, it cannot indefinitely compensate for the absence of solid institutional support. An effective state, sustainable investment, and respectful international cooperation are necessary to transform this resilience into lasting development.
