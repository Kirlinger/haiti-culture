🏥 Public Services

Essential Services in Haiti

The state of fundamental services, access challenges and the urgent needs of the Haitian population.

Home / Essential Services

A Major Challenge

Access to Basic Services

In Haiti, access to essential services remains a daily challenge for a large portion of the population, particularly in rural areas and disadvantaged neighborhoods.

💧

Drinking Water

Access to drinking water is insufficient in many regions. Rural populations often travel long distances to obtain water of questionable quality.

⚡

Electricity

Electricity coverage remains very limited. Port-au-Prince receives only a few hours of electricity per day, and rural areas are often completely without grid access.

🏥

Healthcare

The healthcare system lacks resources, qualified staff and equipment. Public hospitals are often overwhelmed, and private care remains unaffordable for the majority.

📚

Education

Although enrollment has improved, the quality of teaching and dropout rates remain concerning. School fees constitute a barrier for the poorest families.

🚰

Sanitation

Sanitation infrastructure is failing in most cities. The absence of adequate systems promotes the spread of disease and harms quality of life.

🗺️

Territorial Inequalities

The disparities between the capital and the provinces are profound. Rural departments suffer from a glaring lack of public investment and basic infrastructure.

Fundamental Issues

Why Public Services Are Central

Access to essential services is a sine qua non condition of human development. Without quality water, electricity, healthcare and education, it is impossible to break the cycle of poverty and build a stable and prosperous society.
