A Global Legacy

Universal Reach of the Haitian Revolution

The Haitian Revolution (1791–1804) is the only slave revolt in history to have resulted in the founding of an independent state. Its message of freedom and equality inspired emancipation movements throughout the world, from Latin America to Africa.

International Influence

Haiti in World History

Haiti played a decisive role in several major events in the history of the Americas. Alexandre Pétion supported Simón Bolívar in his struggle for South American independence. Haitian ideals nurtured abolitionist movements in the United States and Europe.

Dignity and Sovereignty

Memory, Dignity and Sovereignty

The memory of the Haitian Revolution is intimately linked to human dignity and the sovereignty of peoples. It reminds us that freedom is not given — it is won — and that all human beings are equal in rights and dignity.

Transmission

Why This Memory Must Be Better Shared

Despite its global historical importance, the Haitian Revolution remains poorly taught in international curricula. Better transmission of this memory would contribute to a fairer and more complete understanding of the history of humanity.
