Bibliography & Sources

Complete directory of historical works, academic studies, archives and official documents used in this research project on Haiti. Haitian sources are prioritized.

Home / Bibliography & Sources

This historical research project draws primarily on Haitian historians and intellectuals. The works of Thomas Madiou, Beaubrun Ardouin, Michel-Rolph Trouillot, Jean Price-Mars and other Haitian specialists form the foundation of this documentation. Complementary foreign sources are also included where they provide recognized academic insight.

Priority to Haitian voices

Haitian Historians & Intellectuals

Foundational works produced by Haitian historians, ethnologists and thinkers who documented and analyzed the history of their nation from within.

Thomas Madiou (1814–1884)

  • Madiou, Thomas. Histoire d'Haïti, 8 vols. Port-au-Prince: Imprimerie de J. Courtois, 1847–1848; reprint: Henri Deschamps, 1987–1991. The first major narrative history of Haiti written by a Haitian historian. Indispensable for the revolutionary and post-independence period (1789–1847).

Beaubrun Ardouin (1796–1865)

  • Ardouin, Beaubrun. Études sur l'Histoire d'Haïti, 11 vols. Paris: Dezobry et Magdeleine, 1853–1860. A reference work covering the colonial and revolutionary history of Haiti. Ardouin, a Haitian senator and diplomat, offers a foremost political and institutional perspective.

Jean Price-Mars (1876–1969)

  • Price-Mars, Jean. Ainsi parla l'oncle : Essais d'ethnographie. Compiègne: Imprimerie de Compiègne, 1928; reprint: Leméac, 1973. Founding work of Haitian indigenism. Price-Mars rehabilitates Vodou and African traditions as constitutive elements of Haitian national identity.
  • Price-Mars, Jean. La République d'Haïti et la République Dominicaine, 2 vols. Port-au-Prince: Collection du Tricinquantenaire, 1953. Analysis of the complex historical relations between the two nations sharing the island of Hispaniola.

Michel-Rolph Trouillot (1949–2012)

  • Trouillot, Michel-Rolph. Silencing the Past: Power and the Production of History. Boston: Beacon Press, 1995. A major theoretical essay on how certain historical events — notably the Haitian Revolution — are systematically minimized or erased from global collective memory.
  • Trouillot, Michel-Rolph. Haiti: State Against Nation — The Origins and Legacy of Duvalierism. New York: Monthly Review Press, 1990. Analysis of the structure of the Haitian state and its internal contradictions from the colonial period to Duvalierism.
  • Trouillot, Michel-Rolph. Peasants and Capital: Dominica in the World Economy. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press, 1988.

Hénock Trouillot (1923–1988)

  • Trouillot, Hénock. Historiographie d'Haïti. Mexico: Instituto Panamericano de Geografía e Historia, 1953. Critical synthesis of Haitian historiography from the earliest chronicles to the mid-twentieth century. An essential methodological reference.
  • Trouillot, Hénock. Introduction à une histoire du Vaudou. Port-au-Prince: Imprimerie des Antilles, 1970.

Jacques Nicolas Léger (1859–1918)

  • Léger, Jacques Nicolas. Haiti: Her History and Her Detractors. New York: The Neale Publishing Company, 1907. A documented defence of Haitian history against foreign detractors, written by a Haitian diplomat and jurist. A valuable testimony on Haiti's international relations at the turn of the twentieth century.

Jean Fouchard (1912–1990)

  • Fouchard, Jean. Les Marrons de la Liberté. Paris: L'École, 1972; reprint: Henri Deschamps, 1988. Foundational study of marronage in Saint-Domingue — the resistance of fugitive enslaved people — as a form of resistance preceding and foreshadowing the Revolution of 1791.
  • Fouchard, Jean. La Méringue : Danse nationale d'Haïti. Montréal: Leméac, 1973. Musical and social history of Haitian méringue.

Jean Casimir (born 1935)

  • Casimir, Jean. The Haitians: A Decolonial History. Chapel Hill: University of North Carolina Press, 2020. A revisiting of Haitian history from a decolonial perspective, foregrounding the agency of the popular masses and peasants in the construction of the nation.
  • Casimir, Jean. La Culture Opprimée. Delmas: Lakay, 2001.

Suzy Castor (born 1936)

  • Castor, Suzy. L'Occupation américaine d'Haïti. Port-au-Prince: Société haïtienne d'histoire et de géographie, 1988. In-depth analysis of the American military occupation (1915–1934), its causes, course and lasting consequences for Haitian political and economic structures.
  • Castor, Suzy. La Transition haïtienne : Entre l'épreuve démocratique et l'instabilité. Port-au-Prince: CRESFED, 1996.

Laënnec Hurbon (born 1940)

  • Hurbon, Laënnec. Le Barbare Imaginaire. Port-au-Prince: Henri Deschamps, 1987. Study of Vodou and representations of the Other in Haitian society, analyzing mechanisms of cultural stigmatization.
  • Hurbon, Laënnec. Voodoo: Search for the Spirit. New York: Harry N. Abrams, 1995.
  • Hurbon, Laënnec. Les Mystères du vaudou. Paris: Gallimard / Découvertes, 1993.

International academic research

Academic & International Sources

Works by non-Haitian authors recognized by the academic community for their rigour on Haitian history, society or politics.

Colonial and Revolutionary History

  • James, C. L. R. The Black Jacobins: Toussaint L'Ouverture and the San Domingo Revolution. London: Secker & Warburg, 1938; reprint: Vintage Books, 1989. Classic Marxist analysis of the Haitian Revolution, presenting Toussaint Louverture as a revolutionary figure in the global context of the eighteenth century.
  • Fick, Carolyn. The Making of Haiti: The Saint Domingue Revolution from Below. Knoxville: University of Tennessee Press, 1990. Analysis of the role of ordinary enslaved people in the revolution, beyond the military leaders alone.
  • Dubois, Laurent. Avengers of the New World: The Story of the Haitian Revolution. Cambridge: Harvard University Press, 2004.
  • Moreau de Saint-Méry, M. L. E. Description topographique, physique, civile, politique et historique de la partie française de l'isle Saint-Domingue, 2 vols. Philadelphia, 1797–1798. Major colonial primary source describing Saint-Domingue on the eve of the revolution. Primary source

Contemporary Politics, Society and Economy

  • Lundahl, Mats. Peasants and Poverty: A Study of Haiti. London: Croom Helm, 1979.
  • Farmer, Paul. The Uses of Haiti. Monroe: Common Courage Press, 1994. Analysis of foreign interventions in Haiti and their humanitarian consequences.
  • Farmer, Paul. AIDS and Accusation: Haiti and the Geography of Blame. Berkeley: University of California Press, 1992.

Religion and Culture

  • Métraux, Alfred. Le Vaudou haïtien. Paris: Gallimard, 1958. English translation: Voodoo in Haiti, New York: Oxford University Press, 1959. Reference anthropological study of Haitian Vodou, based on fieldwork conducted in the 1940s–1950s.
  • Brown, Karen McCarthy. Mama Lola: A Vodou Priestess in Brooklyn. Berkeley: University of California Press, 1991.

Primary documents

Official Documents & Archives

Constitutions of Haiti

  • Haitian Constitution of 1801 (Constitution of Toussaint Louverture). Primary source
  • Haitian Constitution of 1805 (Imperial Constitution of Dessalines) — the first constitutional text of the Republic of Haiti, prohibiting white people from owning land. Primary source
  • Haitian Constitution of 1987 — the democratic constitution currently in force, adopted on 29 March 1987 by popular referendum after the fall of the Duvalier dictatorship. Primary source

Founding Historical Acts

  • Act of Independence of Haiti, 1 January 1804. Drafted by Louis Félix Boisrond-Tonnerre at the request of Jean-Jacques Dessalines. National Archives of Haiti, Port-au-Prince. Primary source
  • Treaty of Ryswick, 1697 — Cession of the western part of Saint-Domingue by Spain to France. International treaty
  • Ordinance of Charles X, 17 April 1825 — French recognition of Haitian independence in exchange for an indemnity of 150 million gold francs. Archives of the French Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Paris. Official document
  • Knapp-Forbes Convention (American occupation treaty), 1915. International treaty

Institutional Archives

  • National Archives of Haiti (ANH), Port-au-Prince. Main archival repository of the Republic of Haiti: civil records, notarial registers, government holdings.
  • National Library of Haiti (BNH), Port-au-Prince. Collections of Haitian periodicals, manuscripts and rare printed works.
  • Library of Congress, Washington D.C. — Haitian Collection. Important collection of documents relating to Haitian history, accessible online via loc.gov.
  • Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture, New York Public Library. Haiti holdings: photographs, manuscripts, historical documents on the African diaspora in the Americas.
  • Archives nationales d'outre-mer (ANOM), Aix-en-Provence, France. Colonial holdings from Saint-Domingue: slave registers, administrative documents from the colonial era.

Periodical research

Journals & Academic Publications

  • Revue de la Société Haïtienne d'Histoire et de Géographie. Port-au-Prince, founded 1925. Leading Haitian academic publication in history and geography.
  • Journal of Haitian Studies. Santa Barbara: University of California, founded 1995. Bilingual (French/English) academic journal devoted to Haitian studies.
  • Cahiers des Amériques Latines. Paris: Institut des Hautes Études de l'Amérique Latine (IHEAL). Numerous articles on Haiti.
  • Small Axe: A Caribbean Journal of Criticism. Duke University Press. Caribbean journal including studies on Haitian society and culture.
  • Latin American Research Review. Cambridge University Press. Regular articles on Haitian history and politics.
