Flavors of Haiti

Introduction to Haitian Cuisine

Haitian cuisine is bold, generous and deeply rooted in African and Caribbean traditions. Shaped by centuries of history, it uses fresh local ingredients and a carefully balanced blend of spices. A Haitian meal is always a celebration of life and heritage.

The Essential Dishes

The Haitian Table

Recipes passed down from generation to generation, steeped in history and incomparable flavors.

🍲

Pumpkin soup eaten every January 1st

Soup Joumou

This royal soup made with giraumon (pumpkin), beef, vegetables, pasta and spices was reserved for slave masters during the colonial era. After independence, Haitians claimed it as a symbol of freedom. Enjoyed every January 1st, it is now inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list.

🥩

Fried pork marinated in citrus

Griyo

One of Haiti's most popular dishes: pork marinated in citrus juice and Haitian spices, slowly braised then fried until crispy and golden. Often served with pikliz and rice and beans.

🍚

Rice and beans, the everyday staple

Diri ak Pwa

The iconic combination of Haitian cuisine. Rice is cooked with red or black beans, coconut milk, aromatic herbs and garlic. A comforting, nutritious dish deeply rooted in Haitian culinary tradition.

🍄

Rice with black mushrooms from the North

Diri ak Djon Djon

A festive dish prepared with dried black mushrooms native to northern Haiti, which give the rice its characteristic black color and a unique earthy fragrance. This dish is served on grand occasions and family celebrations.

🌶️

Essential spicy condiment

Pikliz

Vegetables pickled in vinegar with cabbage, carrots, onions and Haitian scotch bonnet peppers. Pikliz is the quintessential condiment of Haitian cuisine, accompanying almost every dish. Beware — its heat can surprise the uninitiated!

🍗

Chicken braised in a rich tomato sauce

Poulet en Sauce

Chicken braised in a rich sauce of tomatoes, peppers, onions and Haitian spices. The quintessential Sunday family dish. The thick, fragrant sauce is ideal spooned over plain white rice.

🍌

Twice-fried plantains

Bannann Peze

Rounds of green plantain fried once, flattened, then returned to the oil until golden and crispy. A universally loved side dish that accompanies all Haitian meals.

🥙

Malanga fritters with pepper

Akra

Crispy fritters made from grated malanga (taro), hot pepper and aromatic herbs. A staple of Haitian street food and a popular starter at parties and gatherings.

🥬

Thick stew of vegetables and meat

Légim

A nutritious and flavorful stew prepared with eggplant, cabbage, carrots, watercress and meat. A traditional dish that showcases the richness of local vegetables and Haitian culinary expertise.

🍌

Green banana porridge, traditional breakfast

Labouyi Bannann

A creamy porridge made from grated green bananas, milk, cinnamon and vanilla. A traditional Haitian breakfast, nourishing and fragrant, particularly beloved by children.

Beverages

Haitian Drinks

🥃

Rhum Barbancourt

Haitian Barbancourt rum is praised by connoisseurs the world over for its finesse and aromatic complexity. Produced since 1862 using French distillation methods, it is Haiti's gastronomic ambassador.

🥤

Kola-Champagne

Haiti's national soft drink, with its sweet and slightly vanilla flavor, is present at every party and family gathering. An indispensable element of Haitian popular culture.

🌿

Sugarcane Juice

Freshly pressed sugarcane juice is a refreshing street drink sold throughout the country. Naturally sweet and energizing, it is a treat accessible to everyone.

☕

Haitian Coffee

Grown in the mountains of the southern part of the country, Haitian coffee is exceptional in richness and aroma. Little known internationally, it fully deserves its reputation among connoisseurs.
