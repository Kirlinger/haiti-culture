The Constitution of Haiti

Discover the legal and democratic foundations that guide the Republic of Haiti.

Akèy / Konstitisyon

Foundations of the Republic

Introduction

The Haitian Constitution is the supreme law of the Republic. It defines the rights, duties, and organization of the State. Haiti has had several constitutions since its independence. The constitution of 1987, adopted after the fall of the Duvalierist dictatorship, is the one currently in force and establishes a democratic republic.

Genesis

Historical Context

From the first constitution of 1801 drafted under Toussaint Louverture to that of 1805 promulgated by Dessalines, passing through numerous revisions over the centuries, Haiti's constitutional history has been turbulent. The Constitution of 1987 was a decisive turning point after decades of authoritarian rule: it marked the return to a rule of law based on universal suffrage and the separation of powers.

Democratic Foundation

Fundamental Constitutional Principles

🏛️

Republican and Democratic State

Haiti is an indivisible, sovereign, independent, cooperative, free, democratic, and social republic.

⚖️

Separation of Powers

The executive, legislative, and judicial powers are separated and independent of one another, guaranteeing institutional balance.

🕊️

Fundamental Rights

The Constitution guarantees the fundamental rights and freedoms of every citizen, including freedom of expression, religion, and association.

🤝

Pluralism and Tolerance

Political, cultural, and religious pluralism is recognized and protected. No discrimination based on race, gender, or religion is permitted.

🗺️

Decentralization

Administrative and territorial decentralization aims to bring the State closer to its citizens and to give local communities the means for their development.

✊

Abolition of Privileges

The Constitution expressly prohibits any privilege based on birth, titles, or hereditary distinctions, affirming the equality of all before the law.

Citizenship

Rights and Duties of Citizens

The Constitution recognizes for every citizen the right to life, liberty, and security. Among the fundamental rights are equality before the law, freedom of expression, freedom of worship, the right to education, and the right to work. In return, every citizen has the duty to respect the law, pay taxes, and contribute to the life of the nation.

Architecture of the State

Organization of the Haitian State

👑

Executive Branch

President and Prime Minister

The President of the Republic is elected by universal suffrage for a five-year term. He shares executive power with the Prime Minister, who is responsible to Parliament.

📜

Legislative Branch

Senate and Chamber of Deputies

The bicameral Parliament comprises the Senate and the Chamber of Deputies. It passes laws, adopts the budget, and oversees government action.

🔨

Judicial Branch

Court of Cassation

The Court of Cassation is the highest court in the country. The judicial system safeguards the rights of citizens and the application of the law throughout the territory.
