Founding documents, historical speeches and texts that forged Haitian identity.

Akèy / Achiv

Memory and Identity

Introduction to the Historical Archives

These digital archives bring together founding documents, historical texts and essential speeches relating to Haiti. These texts form the foundation of Haitian identity and memory. They bear witness to the struggle for freedom, the building of the state and the voice of those who shaped the nation.

Founding Texts

Historical Documents

📜

January 1st, 1804

Act of Independence

The official declaration of independence signed by Dessalines and the revolutionary leaders. The original was presumed lost, but a copy was rediscovered in 2010 at the British National Archives, causing immense emotion in Haiti and around the world.

📜

Toussaint Louverture

Constitution of 1801

The first constitution of Saint-Domingue, drafted under the authority of Toussaint Louverture. It abolished slavery throughout the territory and named Toussaint governor for life — an act of extraordinary political audacity in the colonial context of the time.

📜

Under Emperor Dessalines

Constitution of 1805

The first constitution of independent Haiti, drafted under the authority of Emperor Dessalines. This founding text declared all Haitians "Black," regardless of their skin color — a radical and deliberate break from the racist ideology of colonization.

📜

1804

Boisrond-Tonnerre Proclamation

The official text of the declaration of independence, written by Boisrond-Tonnerre with deliberate revolutionary ferocity. This document constitutes a total and definitive break with colonial France and a call to the dignity of all oppressed peoples.

📣

November 29, 1803

Act of Gonaïves

Formal act proclaiming the independence of the western part of Hispaniola, signed one month before the official date of independence. This document foreshadows the birth of the Haitian nation and seals the irreversible victory of the Revolution.

📣

1804

Speeches of Jean-Jacques Dessalines

Major speeches by the founding father calling for national unity, freedom and Haitian pride. These texts reveal the political vision and determination of a man who broke forever with the colonial order.

Voice of the Nation

Important Speeches and Texts

📣

Henri Christophe

Speech on Nation-Building (1811)

Henri I develops his vision of a prosperous and sovereign Haitian state, capable of demonstrating to the world the capacity of Black people to govern themselves and build a great civilization.

📣

Alexandre Pétion

On Democracy and Land Reform (1809)

Pétion sets out the foundations of his progressive social policy, including the redistribution of land to former enslaved people and soldiers of the Revolution.

📣

Jean Price-Mars

Thus Spoke the Uncle (1928)

Founding text of the Haitian Indigenist movement. Price-Mars calls on Haitian intellectuals to value African cultures and the Vodou tradition as the foundation of an authentic national identity.

📣

Jean-Bertrand Aristide

First Inaugural Address (1991)

Haiti's first democratically elected president, Aristide expresses his vision of a just and equitable Haiti, founded on popular participation and the dignity of all citizens.

Resources

Consulting the Original Archives

To access the original documents, researchers can consult the National Archives of Haiti in Port-au-Prince as well as the British National Archives in London, where a copy of the 1804 Act of Independence was found in 2010. Many American and European universities also hold collections of Haitian historical documents.
