🏛️ Heritage

Celebrating National Heritage

Haiti's cultural, historical, and natural riches — a precious legacy for future generations.

Akèy / National Heritage

A Treasure to Preserve

Haitian Heritage

Haiti possesses an exceptional cultural and natural heritage that deserves to be known, protected, and celebrated — both pou Ayitians and for the world at large.

🏰

UNESCO Heritage

The Citadelle Laferrière, the Sans-Souci Palace, and the Ramiers together form the National Historic Park, listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982.

🎨

Art and Culture

Haitian naïve painting, metal sculptures, Vodou music, and Creole literature constitute an artistic heritage of extraordinary richness.

🌿

Natural Heritage

Haiti's mountain ranges, coastal areas, and biodiversity — despite deforestation — represent a natural heritage that deserves protection and restoration.

📜

Historical Archives

The founding documents of the Republic, along with colonial and revolutionary archives, constitute an irreplaceable memory for understanding Haitian and world history.

🥘

Culinary Heritage

Haitian cuisine, notably Soup Joumou recognized by UNESCO, is an intangible cultural heritage that tells the story of independence and freedom.

🥁

Living Traditions

Vodou, Rara, Carnival, and oral traditions are living heritages that continue to be practiced and passed down from generation to generation.

🇭🇹 Haïti 🇭🇹

A celebration of Haiti's extraordinary history, its vibrant culture and its remarkable people. Dedicated to sharing the story of the first Black Republic in the world.
