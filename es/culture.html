A Crossroads of Civilizations

Introduction to Haitian Culture

Haitian culture is a rich and unique blend of African, French, Taíno and Caribbean influences. It expresses itself through vibrant art, spellbinding music, expressive dance, powerful literature and the traditions of everyday life. Despite constant economic challenges, Haitian creativity has earned well-deserved international recognition.

Sound and Rhythm

Haitian Music

🎵

Kompa

The national popular music created in the 1950s by Nemours Jean-Baptiste, Kompa (or Compas) is the quintessential festive rhythm of Haitians, danced at every celebration.

🥁

Rara

A ritual processional music played during Lent, Rara blends singing, percussion and wind instruments in parades that wind through the streets and country roads.

🌿

Rasin

Roots music (Rasin) combines the sacred rhythms of Vodou with modern instruments. A powerful genre that celebrates the African roots of Haitian culture.

🎸

Twoubadou

The Haitian troubadour tradition offers romantic and melancholic songs, often accompanied by guitar, that celebrate love, nature and everyday life.

Visual Expression

Haitian Art, Celebrated Around the World

Haitian painting is recognized worldwide for its naïve or primitive style, its vivid colors and its striking images of spiritual and daily life. The Centre d'Art in Port-au-Prince, founded in 1944, propelled Haitian artists onto the international stage. Masters such as Hector Hyppolite, a Vodou priest and visionary painter, Philomé Obin and Castera Bazile defined the identity of Haitian art. The Cathedral of the Holy Trinity in Port-au-Prince houses famous frescoes. Metal sculpture cut from oil drums is another Haitian art form appreciated worldwide.

Body and Soul

Haitian Dance

🕊️

Yanvalou

The sacred dance of Vodou, Yanvalou imitates the undulations of the sacred serpent Damballah. It is a dance of deep devotion, practiced during ritual ceremonies.

🎭

Haitian Méringue

The Haitian national dance, the Haitian méringue is distinguished from its Dominican cousin by its own rhythms and movements, deeply rooted in popular tradition.

🎊

Rara Dances

Associated with the Rara processions of Lent, these energetic and expressive dances accompany musical groups in their festive and ritual parades.

Words and Letters

Haitian Literature

Haiti has an exceptionally rich literary tradition. Jean Price-Mars founded the Indigenism movement in the 1920s, celebrating the African roots of Haitian culture. Jacques Roumain wrote the foundational novel Masters of the Dew (Gouverneurs de la Rosée, 1944), a work of universal significance. Edwidge Danticat is the most widely read Haitian author in the English-speaking world. Haitian literature exists in both French and Haitian Creole, two languages that coexist and enrich one another.

Celebration and Identity

Haitian Carnival

Haitian Carnival is one of the most spectacular celebrations in the Caribbean. Held before Lent, it gathers millions of people around decorated floats, elaborate costumes, massive musical groups and an unmatched collective energy. Each city has its own traditions: the Port-au-Prince Carnival is the largest, while the Jacmel Carnival is renowned for its handcrafted papier-mâché masks.

Identity and Expression

The Creole Language

Haitian Creole (Kreyòl ayisyen) is spoken by virtually all Haitians. Born during the colonial period from the fusion of French with West African languages, it has been one of the country's two official languages since 1987. French is the other official language, used in administration and formal education. Literature and music in Creole have experienced remarkable growth, giving this language a cultural identity that is proudly claimed.
