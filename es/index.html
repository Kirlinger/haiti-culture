A Nation Like No Other

The Heart of the Antilles

Haiti, officially the Republic of Haiti, occupies the western third of the island of Hispaniola in the Caribbean Sea. Sharing the island with the Dominican Republic, Haiti has over 11 million inhabitants and a rich cultural heritage shaped by African, French and indigenous Taino influences.

Haitian Creole (Kreyòl ayisyen) and French are the official languages. The people are known for their warmth, artistic talent and extraordinary resilience in the face of adversity.

  • Capital: Port-au-Prince
  • Official languages: Haitian Creole & French
  • Independence declared: January 1, 1804
  • First Black Republic in the world

Why Visit Haiti

Unique Experiences Await You

Haiti offers a rare blend of history, nature, art and authentic culture that you will find nowhere else in the Caribbean.

🏰

World Heritage

The Citadelle Laferrière and the Sans-Souci Palace are world heritage sites and symbols of Haiti's glorious revolutionary past.

🎶

Vibrant Music

Kompa, Rara and Rasin music fill the streets. Haitian music has influenced Caribbean and world music for centuries.

🎨

World-Renowned Art

Haitian art is celebrated internationally. From naïve paintings to intricate metal sculptures, Haitian artists captivate the world.

🌊

Beautiful Beaches

The beaches of Labadee, Île-à-Vache and Jacmel offer pristine sands and turquoise waters rivaling any Caribbean destination.

🍽️

Delicious Cuisine

Griyo, diri ak djon djon, pikliz, Haitian cuisine is bold, flavorful and deeply rooted in African and Caribbean traditions.

🎭

Festivals & Carnival

Haitian Carnival is one of the most spectacular in the Caribbean. Rara festivals bring communities together in joyful celebration.
