📊 Economy

Economic Fragility in Haiti

An analysis of structural economic challenges and pathways toward sustainable development.

Inicio / Economic Fragility

Diagnosis

Characteristics of Economic Fragility

The Haitian economy is characterized by a strong dependence on diaspora remittances, a limited productive base, vulnerability to natural disasters, and chronic political instability that discourages investment.

Informal Economy

The Weight of Informality

More than 80% of Haitian economic activity takes place in the informal sector. While this sector ensures the survival of millions of people, it also limits the state's capacity to collect tax revenues and fund public services.

Social Impact

Consequences for Households

Economic fragility translates directly into the daily lives of Haitian families: food insecurity, difficulty paying school fees, limited access to healthcare. These realities weaken the country's human capital.

Perspectives

Pathways for Economic Strengthening

Economic diversification, tourism development, support for agriculture, and the gradual formalization of the informal economy are pathways to strengthening Haiti's economic resilience over the long term.
