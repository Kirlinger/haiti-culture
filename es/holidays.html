The Haitian Calendar

National Celebrations & Commemorations

Throughout the year, Haitians celebrate their history, religious traditions and national identity through a rich calendar of official and popular holidays.

📅 January 1st

Independence Day

Haiti's most important national holiday. In 1804, Jean-Jacques Dessalines proclaimed independence at Gonaïves. The day is celebrated with official ceremonies, music and the ritual tasting of soup joumou, once reserved exclusively for the slaveholding masters.

📅 January 2nd

Ancestors' Day

This day is dedicated to the founding fathers and mothers of the nation, to all those who fought and sacrificed their lives so that Haiti could be free. A day of reflection and national remembrance.

📅 January 6th

Epiphany (Three Kings Day)

Catholic celebration of Epiphany, marking the visit of the Magi to the infant Jesus. A religious feast observed throughout the country, with king cakes and family celebrations.

📅 February / March

Carnival

Haiti's greatest popular festival, celebrated during the three days preceding Ash Wednesday. Music, sumptuous costumes, decorated floats and massive crowds make Haitian Carnival one of the most spectacular in the Caribbean.

📅 February / March

Ash Wednesday

The beginning of Catholic Lent. After the exuberance of Carnival, the faithful receive ashes on their foreheads as a sign of penance and preparation for Easter.

📅 April 14th

Pan American Day

Pan-American day commemorating solidarity between the nations of the American continent. It recalls Haiti's pioneering role in the independence struggles of the Americas.

📅 Good Friday

Good Friday

Catholic holy day commemorating the crucifixion of Jesus Christ. A day of reflection, religious processions and intense prayer throughout the country.

📅 May 1st

Labour Day

International Workers' Day, celebrated in Haiti as around the world. Trade union demonstrations and celebrations bring together workers and activists to defend workers' rights.

📅 May 18th

Flag Day

Commemorates the creation of the Haitian flag at the Congress of Arcahaie in 1803, when Dessalines tore the white from the French tricolor banner to keep only the blue and red. A day of great national pride.

📅 August 14th

Bois Caïman Ceremony

Commemoration of the Bois Caïman ceremony of August 14, 1791, the symbolic moment that triggered the Haitian Revolution. A sacred site in the collective Haitian memory.

📅 August 15th

Assumption

Catholic feast of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary. Celebrated with solemn masses and processions throughout the country, it is one of the most important religious feasts of the liturgical year.

📅 October 17th

Death of Dessalines

Day commemorating the assassination of founding father Jean-Jacques Dessalines on October 17, 1806. A day of national mourning in tribute to the man who proclaimed the country's independence.

📅 November 1st

All Saints' Day

Catholic feast of All Saints, honoring all the saints. In Haiti, this day also holds particular importance in the Vodou tradition, which honors the ancestors and the spirits of the deceased.

📅 November 2nd

Day of the Dead

Day of the dead, deeply embedded in Haitian culture. Families visit cemeteries to honor their ancestors. In the Vodou tradition, the Lwa Gede, masters of death, are especially celebrated on this day.

📅 November 18th

Battle of Vertières

Commemoration of the decisive victory of Haitian forces over Napoleon's troops on November 18, 1803. This battle, won under the command of Dessalines and Capois la Mort, made independence irreversible.

📅 December 5th

Discovery of Haiti

Commemoration of Christopher Columbus's arrival on the island on December 5, 1492. This date, which marks the beginning of a new era, is an occasion to reflect on pre-Columbian history and the Taíno heritage.

📅 December 25th

Christmas

Christmas is widely celebrated in Haiti. Families gather, churches are packed for midnight masses and the festive atmosphere fills the country with songs, lights and shared joy.
