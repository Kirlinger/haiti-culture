FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Haiti, its culture, and visiting the country.

Travel

Is Haiti safe to visit?

Like any destination, safety depends on where you go and how you prepare. Many areas of Haiti — especially tourist sites like Labadee, Jacmel, and Cap-Haïtien — are visited regularly. Always check current travel advisories and travel with a reputable local guide.

Visa

Do I need a visa for Haiti?

Citizens of many countries do not need a visa for stays under 90 days. A valid passport and return ticket are typically required. Check your country's official travel information for current requirements.

Language

Do I need to speak French or Creole?

In tourist areas, English is often spoken. French is useful, but learning a few words of Haitian Creole ("Bonjou" = Hello, "Mèsi" = Thank you) will go a long way and delight the locals!

Money

What currency does Haiti use?

Haiti uses the Haitian Gourde (HTG). US Dollars are widely accepted in tourist areas, hotels, and larger establishments. It's good to carry local currency for markets and street vendors.

Culture

What is Vodou exactly?

Haitian Vodou is a legitimate religious system, not the Hollywood stereotype. It honors ancestors and spirits (Lwa) through ritual, music, and dance. It was officially recognized as a religion in Haiti in 2003 and played a key role in the Revolution.

Food

What should I eat in Haiti?

Don't miss griyo (fried pork), diri ak djon djon (black mushroom rice), soup joumou (a symbol of independence), and the spicy condiment pikliz. Fresh tropical fruits and seafood are also exceptional.
