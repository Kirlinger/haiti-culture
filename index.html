A Nation Like No Other

The Heart of the Caribbean

Haiti, officially the Republic of Haiti, occupies the western third of the island of Hispaniola in the Caribbean Sea. Sharing the island with the Dominican Republic, Haiti has a population of over 11 million people and a rich cultural heritage shaped by African, French, and indigenous Taíno influences.

Haitian Creole (Kreyòl ayisyen) and French are the official languages. The people are known for their warmth, artistic talent, and extraordinary resilience in the face of adversity.

  • Capital: Port-au-Prince
  • Official languages: Haitian Creole & French
  • Independence declared: January 1, 1804
  • First Black republic in the world

Why Visit Haiti

Unique Experiences Await

Haiti offers a rare blend of history, nature, art, and authentic culture that you won't find anywhere else in the Caribbean.

🏰

UNESCO Heritage

La Citadelle Laferrière and Sans-Souci Palace are UNESCO World Heritage sites and symbols of Haiti's glorious revolutionary past.

🎶

Vibrant Music

Kompa, Rara, and Rasin music fill the streets. Haitian music has influenced Caribbean and world music for centuries.

🎨

World-Class Art

Haiti's art is internationally celebrated. From naïve paintings to intricate metal sculptures, Haitian artists captivate the world.

🌊

Stunning Beaches

Labadee, Ile-à-Vache, and Jacmel's beaches offer pristine sands and turquoise waters rivaling any Caribbean destination.

🍽️

Delicious Cuisine

Griyo, diri ak djon djon, pikliz — Haitian cuisine is bold, flavorful, and deeply rooted in African and Caribbean traditions.

🎭

Festivals & Carnival

Haitian Carnival is one of the most spectacular in the Caribbean. Rara festivals bring communities together in joyous celebration.

"Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité" — the ideals of the French Revolution were turned into reality by Haitian men and women who dared to be free.
