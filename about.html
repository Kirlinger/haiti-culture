Who We Are

A Nation of Extraordinary People

Haiti, officially the Republic of Haiti (République d'Haïti / Repiblik Ayiti), is a Caribbean country on the western part of the island of Hispaniola. With over 11 million inhabitants, it is the most populated country in the Caribbean.

Haitian people are descendants of enslaved Africans, indigenous Taíno people, and French colonizers. This mix has created a culture that is uniquely Haitian — expressive, resilient, and deeply spiritual.

Port-au-Prince is the capital and largest city. The country is officially bilingual, with Haitian Creole (Kreyòl ayisyen) spoken by the entire population and French used in official and educational contexts.

Language & Expression

Kreyòl — The Language of the Heart

Haitian Creole is more than a language — it is an identity, a form of resistance, and the living proof of Haiti's unique history.

🗣️

Haitian Creole

Spoken by 100% of Haitians, Kreyòl evolved from African languages, French, Spanish, and English. It became an official language in 1987.

📚

French Language

French remains an official language used in government, education, and business — a reminder of Haiti's colonial history.

✍️

Literature

Haiti has a rich literary tradition. Authors like Jacques Roumain, René Depestre, and Edwidge Danticat have achieved international recognition.

📢

Oral Tradition

Storytelling, proverbs (pwovèb), and oral histories have preserved Haitian culture across generations. "Krik? Krak!" opens every story.

🎶

Sound & Rhythm

Music — The Soul of Haiti

Music is inseparable from Haitian life. From sacred Vodou ceremonies to packed dance floors, rhythm runs through everything Haitian.

  • Kompa — Haiti's beloved popular music genre, born in the 1950s
  • Rara — street festival music tied to Lenten celebrations
  • Rasin (Roots) — spiritual music blending Vodou and rock
  • Twoubadou — romantic Haitian folk music with guitar
  • Méringue — the traditional national music genre

Notable artists include Coupé Cloué, Ti Paris, Tabou Combo, and Wyclef Jean, who brought Haitian sounds to global audiences.

🖼️

Visual Arts

Haitian Art — A World Treasure

Haiti's visual arts scene is internationally celebrated. Haitian paintings are known for their bold colors, spiritual themes, and vivid depictions of everyday life.

The Centre d'Art, founded in Port-au-Prince in 1944, launched Haiti's modern art movement. Haitian naïve art gained world attention when artists like Hector Hyppolite and Philomé Obin were discovered.

  • Naïve / Primitivist painting tradition
  • Metal sculpture using repurposed oil drums
  • Papier-mâché masks and carnival arts
  • Vèvè — sacred Vodou symbols used in art
  • Contemporary Haitian artists exhibited worldwide

Food & Flavors

Haitian Cuisine

Bold spices, fresh ingredients, and African culinary traditions come together to create a cuisine that reflects Haiti's rich heritage.

🍖

National Dish

Griyo (Fried Pork)

Marinated pork shoulder fried crispy and served with pikliz (spicy pickled vegetables) and rice. Haiti's beloved national dish.

🍚

Staple Food

Diri ak Djon Djon

Black mushroom rice — one of Haiti's most prized dishes, made with unique djon djon mushrooms that give the rice its distinctive dark color.

🌶️

Condiment

Pikliz

Haiti's essential condiment — a fiery pickled mix of cabbage, carrots, and Scotch bonnet peppers in vinegar. Found at every Haitian table.

Spirituality

Faith & Vodou — The Spiritual Heart

Religion is central to Haitian life. The majority of Haitians are Catholic or Protestant Christians, but many also practice Vodou — a spiritual system brought from West Africa that played a key role in the Revolution of 1791.

Haitian Vodou (not to be confused with Hollywood voodoo) is a rich, complex religion that honors ancestors, spirits (Lwa), and the divine through music, dance, ritual, and ceremony.

  • Bossou, Erzulie, Papa Legba — venerated Lwa spirits
  • Bwa Kayiman ceremony (1791) sparked the Revolution
  • Vodou officially recognized as a religion in 2003
  • Deep connection to healing, community, and nature
